Interests

There are many experiences from when I was young that pushed me to want to give back to that which forged me. For the better part of a decade, including after I became and Eagle Scout, I lived this through teaching methods of leadership in a practical, intensive (possibly trial by fire) course through the Scouts, B.S.A. (previously Boy Scouts of America). It was through scouting, leading, and teaching that I learned how to adapt and work well with people of many backgrounds and utilize their strengths to create something much grander than the sum of their parts.

In my free time, what little I find, I worldbuild a bespoke world for my close friends and use it as a setting for the a long running (read as 7 years) Dungeons and Dragons campaign. This long form story has given me a unique opportunity to watch my own growth as a story teller and worldbuilder grow and deepen. Some of my favourite parts of that worldbuilding is being able to know my world and its cultures well enough that I can create characters on the fly and be rest assured that I'll be able to fit them in the world very well.

I'm a forever learner. The more novel challenges I can present myself with the better. The fun for me is always in the puzzle box nature of learning what exactly I'm missing, adapting to the new information, and continuing to break the problem down until I've finally found the root and can begin to build the solution on piece at a time.

While it's not the easiest thing to do a lot of, I love to travel. The sheer amount you can learn from living amongst different cultures or just existing out in the woods away from society is astounding. It opens up new perspectives and reveals assumptions made about others that are more archetype than reality. This only helps in bringing my own personal worldbuilding to life and helps me see the world in new and exciting ways.
